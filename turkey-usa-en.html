Turkey vs. USA: Preview and Prediction for the 2026 World Cup Match (26.06.2026)

On Friday, June 26, 2026, the final matchday of the FIFA World Cup Group D will feature an intriguing encounter between the national teams of Turkey and the United States. The game will take place at the state-of-the-art SoFi Stadium (Los Angeles Stadium) in Inglewood, California, USA. Kickoff is scheduled for 05:00 Kyiv time (June 25, 19:00 local PDT). Although both teams' fates have already been decided, this low-stakes tactical battle promises to be highly entertaining.

Group D Standings and Motivation

Ahead of the final round, the table of Group D stands as follows:

  • 1. USA — 6 Points (Goal difference: +5, Goals: 6:1)
  • 2. Australia — 3 Points (Goal difference: 0, Goals: 2:2)
  • 3. Paraguay — 3 Points (Goal difference: -2, Goals: 2:4)
  • 4. Turkey — 0 Points (Goal difference: -3, Goals: 0:3)

Mauricio Pochettino's USA side has already secured first place in the group and booked a spot in the Round of 32 thanks to back-to-back victories over Paraguay (4-1) and Australia (2-0). Conversely, Vincenzo Montella's Turkey suffered two successive defeats without scoring a single goal (0-2 to Australia, 0-1 to Paraguay), leading to their mathematical elimination from the tournament.

Consequently, this match will serve as an experimental stage. The United States can afford to rotate their squad, manage player fatigue, and fine-tune tactical details ahead of the knockout rounds. For Turkey, this match is a matter of pride and honor—an opportunity to end their World Cup campaign on a high note and gift their passionate traveling fans a goal to celebrate.

Turkey: Playing for Pride with a Generational Core

The Turkish national team enters this match under a cloud of disappointment. After a 24-year wait to return to the World Cup stage, their quick exit has left fans deflated. Their biggest struggle has been offensive efficiency; despite creating several promising opportunities, Turkey failed to find the net in both of their opening games.

Vincenzo Montella is expected to field Turkey's young, generational talents. Much of the spotlight will be on Real Madrid's Arda Güler and Juventus's Kenan Yildiz. Playing without the psychological pressure of qualification, these young stars will look to showcase their quality on the global stage.

USA: Pochettino's Tactical Adjustments and Rising Stars

The USMNT under Mauricio Pochettino has looked sharp, athletic, and highly energetic. Their high-pressing style and rapid wing play have yielded great results. Villarreal's fullback Alex Freeman has been the absolute breakout star of the American defense, racking up 1 goal (the match-winner against Australia) and 1 assist in just two games.

While Pochettino will likely rotate the starting lineup to rest key figures, in-form attackers like Ricardo Pepi, Christian Pulisic, and Folarin Balogun are expected to feature to maintain their momentum and sharp competitive rhythm before entering the knockout stage.

Head-to-Head History (H2H)

Turkey and the USA have faced each other five times in friendly and official matches. Remarkably, all previous five meetings produced goals at both ends (Both Teams to Score - Yes). Their last official encounter came in the 2003 Confederations Cup, where Turkey edged out a 2-1 victory.

Turkey vs. USA: Expert Prediction

With both sides freed from the pressure of qualifying, fans can expect an open, attacking, and highly engaging football match. Turkey will pour everything into scoring their first World Cup goal and avoiding a third straight defeat. The USA, supported by a massive crowd at SoFi Stadium, will aim to keep their winning momentum alive.

The head-to-head history and current team profiles heavily point toward goals on both sides. While Turkey's defense remains vulnerable, their creative core with Güler and Çalhanoğlu is more than capable of breaking down a rotated American backline.

Recommended Bet: Both Teams to Score (Yes) at 1.63 odds or Over 2.5 goals. The predicted score is a 1-1 draw or a 2-1 victory for the USA.
