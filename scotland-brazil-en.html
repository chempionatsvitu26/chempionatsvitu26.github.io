Scotland vs. Brazil: Preview and Prediction for the 2026 World Cup Match (25.06.2026)

In the early hours of June 25, 2026, one of the most highly anticipated matches of the FIFA World Cup group stage in Group C will take place. At the Hard Rock Stadium (Miami Gardens, USA), the national teams of Scotland and Brazil will face off. The kickoff is scheduled for 01:00 Kyiv time (June 24 at 18:00 local Miami time). This encounter is of crucial importance for both teams on their path to the knockout stage.

Group C Standings and Scenarios

Ahead of the final matchday, the situation in Group C remains highly intriguing. Brazil leads the group with 4 points after a 1-1 draw with Morocco and a comfortable 3-0 victory over Haiti. Morocco also has 4 points but trails the Seleção on goal difference.

Scotland currently sits in third place with 3 points, following a 1-0 win against Haiti and a narrow 0-1 defeat to Morocco. To guarantee their place in the Round of 32, the "Tartan Army" needs to secure at least a draw against Brazil. Even in the event of a defeat, Scotland would still maintain a strong chance of progressing as one of the best third-placed teams (Opta analysts rate this probability at 74%). However, Steve Clarke's squad will surely aim to decide their own fate by obtaining a positive result.

Scotland: Discipline and Counter-Attacking Hopes

Scotland has returned to the World Cup stage for the first time since 1998 and has already demonstrated its competitive spirit. Head coach Steve Clarke is expected to opt for a compact defensive shape with a low block, aiming to deny space to the Brazilian wingers.

Key figures for the Scots in this matchup will be Andy Robertson, Scott McTominay, John McGinn, and striker Che Adams. The Tartan Army understands they will likely spend most of the match defending, which makes set-piece execution and quick counter-attacks vital to their hopes.

Brazil: Neymar's Return and Squad Concerns

The Brazilian national team under Carlo Ancelotti is gradually finding its rhythm in the tournament, though their 3-0 victory over Haiti came at a cost. Barcelona winger Raphinha suffered a hamstring injury and is ruled out of the game against Scotland.

On a positive note, the head coach confirmed that superstar forward Neymar has fully recovered from his injury, has trained with the main squad, and is ready to feature (likely off the bench). Alongside Vinicius Junior, Gabriel Martinelli, and the young Endrick, the Seleção’s attack remains incredibly formidable even in Raphinha's absence.

World Cup Head-to-Head History (H2H)

The clash between Scotland and Brazil is a classic World Cup group-stage fixture. This will be the fifth meeting between the two sides at this stage in World Cup history (previous encounters took place in 1974, 1982, 1990, and 1998). Their last World Cup meeting was in 1998, where Brazil secured a hard-fought 2-1 victory.
