Paraguay vs. Australia: Preview and Prediction for the Decisive 2026 World Cup Match (26.06.2026)

On Friday, June 26, 2026, a crucial Group D encounter of the FIFA World Cup 2026 will take place. The national teams of Paraguay and Australia will go head-to-head at the San Francisco Bay Area Stadium (Levi's Stadium) in Santa Clara, California, USA. Kickoff is scheduled for 05:00 Kyiv time (June 25, 19:00 local PDT / 20:00 Paraguay time). The match will be officiated by the experienced French referee Clément Turpin. This clash represents a direct shootout for second place in the group and a ticket to the knockout rounds.

Group D Standings and Qualification Scenarios

Ahead of the final group stage fixture, the margin for error is razor-thin. The United States have already claimed first place in Group D with 6 points, while Turkey have been eliminated. The race for second place and automatic progression to the Round of 32 will be decided between today's opponents:

  • 1. United States — 6 points (Goal difference: 6:1)
  • 2. Australia — 3 points (Goal difference: 2:2)
  • 3. Paraguay — 3 points (Goal difference: 2:4)
  • 4. Turkey — 0 points (Goal difference: 0:3)

The math is simple. Thanks to a superior goal difference (0 compared to Paraguay's -2), Australia need only a draw to secure second place and automatic progression. Paraguay, on the other hand, must win to guarantee their place in the Round of 32. In the event of a draw, the South Americans would finish third on 4 points, leaving them to rely on the third-place teams' ranking—a highly risky scenario given their negative goal difference.

Paraguay: Missing Almirón and Relying on Guaraní Grit

Gustavo Alfaro's Paraguay have experienced mixed momentum at this World Cup. Following a tough 1-4 opening defeat to the US, they responded with a gutsy 1-0 win over Turkey. However, that vital victory came at a massive cost: their star playmaker Miguel Almirón received a red card in the first half and is suspended for this decisive match.

In Almirón's absence, and with Ramón Sosa doubtful due to muscle discomfort, the creative burden will fall on the shoulders of Julio Enciso and Matías Galarza, who scored the stunning match-winner against Turkey. Paraguay's tactical style relies heavily on a compact, stubborn defense anchored by captain Gustavo Gómez. Since most of their recent victories have come by a single-goal margin, they will likely adopt a highly pragmatic approach once again.

Australia: Selection Dilemmas and Defensive Resilience

Tony Popovic's Australia have enjoyed a steadier campaign. They opened with a controlled, disciplined 2-0 win over Turkey before falling to a 2-0 defeat against group winners USA. The Socceroos' squad features a robust blend of tournament experience and exciting youth.

The primary selection dilemma for Popovic revolves around his attacking line. Fans and pundits are calling for teenage star Nestory Irankunda to start, following his electric goal-scoring display off the bench on Matchday 1. The situation is complicated by a minor muscle injury to veteran winger Mathew Leckie. It is highly likely Popovic will rest the 35-year-old to avoid risking him ahead of the knockout phase, with Cristian Volpato likely to start instead. Defensively, the Socceroos look solid with giant Harry Souttar protecting goalkeeper Patrick Beach.

Head-to-Head History (H2H)

This will be the first-ever World Cup meeting between Paraguay and Australia. Historically, they have met in five friendly games, and Australia remain undefeated against the South American side (2 wins, 3 draws). Their last encounter in 2010 ended in a narrow 1-0 victory for the Socceroos.

Paraguay vs. Australia: Expert Prediction

Given the massive stakes and the pragmatic natures of both managers, this match is highly unlikely to turn into an open, high-scoring affair. Since a draw is enough for Australia, Tony Popovic will almost certainly set up a defensive block, aiming to maintain defensive shape and strike on the counter.

The suspension of Miguel Almirón seriously blunts Paraguay's creative spark in the final third. Without their key playmaker, breaking down a well-organized Australian defense marshaled by the towering Harry Souttar will be an uphill battle for the South Americans. Expect a gritty, physical battle in the midfield with plenty of tackles.

Recommended Bet: Under 2.5 goals at 1.65 odds, or the double chance "Draw or Australia (X2)" at 1.44 odds. The expected final scoreline is a 0-0 stalemate or a tight 0-1 win for the Socceroos.
